Formula 1 Podium Prediction Model
Elastic Net · Classification · Sports Analytics
ML
Built a machine learning model to predict which drivers will finish on the podium before the race begins, using over 5,000 race-driver observations from the Ergast F1 dataset (2012–2024).
  • Engineered features from qualifying grid position, driver age, constructor, nationality, pit strategy (laps per pit), and circuit characteristics (lat, lng, altitude).
  • Developed an Elastic Net classification model that correctly identified at least one podium finisher 76.2% of the time.
  • Found that grid position is the single strongest predictor of podium outcomes, more impactful than nationality, constructor, or pit strategy (supported by coefficient plots on slides 11, 13, and 30).
  • Compared model performance to baseline strategies—predicting grid positions 1–3 yields 100% “at least one podium” accuracy but fails to assign probabilities.

Housing Price Prediction in Fortune 500 Cities
Regression · Regularization · Market Analytics
ML
Analyzed housing prices across 335 U.S. cities to understand whether the presence of Fortune 500 companies affects median home prices, using Zillow and FBI UCR datasets.
  • Performed multiple linear regression with log transformations to correct skewness (see slide 7 histograms).
  • Controlled for population size and violent crime, testing both continuous and categorical Fortune 500 variables.
  • Found no statistically significant relationship between Fortune 500 presence and housing prices (coefficient CI plot on slide 9).
  • Identified population and violent crime as the strongest drivers of median home prices, not corporate presence.
  • Recommended markets: Atlanta (affordable with growth potential) and San Francisco (high-value, high price).
